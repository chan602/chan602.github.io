What are the Three Wins?

Each day, I aim to achieve three wins: a physical, mental, and spiritual win. This framework has circulated online in various forms, and I adopted it because it aligns with my goal of being intentional across multiple dimensions of well-being. The definitions are intentionally flexible; what constitutes a “spiritual win” for me might be completely different for someone else. At its core, the system emphasizes three daily themes that make me feel fulfilled, accomplished, and well, beyond just being productive.

For years, I relied on handwritten to-do lists. I later experimented with tracking tasks with ChatGPT, hoping AI could track my tasks conveniently and explicitly evaluate three wins based on my personal parameters. However, I found that traditional to-do systems require consistent, structured logic, while AI is not well-suited to maintain that level of reliability. Tasks were sometimes misinterpreted, inconsistently tracked, or lost entirely. While the evaluation aspect was valuable, the system itself was not dependable.

This led me to create a hybrid solution: a structured, logic-driven task system paired with AI for evaluation rather than task management. After an extensive iterative effort, designing, debugging, and optimizing, I developed a system that reliably tracks and archives daily and weekly tasks, while leveraging the Anthropic API to evaluate progress and identify wins. I'm really proud of this build, and I'm working on scaling it into something I could use with friends.

UPDATE: The multi-user version of this page has been developed! I've moved my todo lists to the app I built, and as such this page is no longer live. You can now view my daily wins at my profile in the app.
